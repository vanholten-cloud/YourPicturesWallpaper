# YourPicturesWallpaper — Support

YourPicturesWallpaper is a macOS app that turns your own photos into your desktop wallpaper. Pick a folder, and the app rotates through the images at the interval you choose.

This page is the official support page for the app. If you're stuck or have a question, the fastest way to reach me is by email.

- macOS 13 (Ventura) or later
- About 50 MB of free disk space
- Permission to access the folder you want to use as a wallpaper source

## Frequently asked questions

### How do I get started?

After installing, open YourPicturesWallpaper. The first time you run it, macOS will ask whether the app may access the folder you choose (Desktop, Documents, Downloads, or Photos). Allow access, pick a folder of images, and set how often you'd like the wallpaper to change. That's it.

### Why does the app ask for access to my Desktop / Documents / Downloads folder?

It needs permission to read the images you want to use as wallpaper. The app only reads the folder you select — it does not upload, copy, or modify your files. You can revoke access at any time in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Files and Folders.

### Why does it ask for Photos / media library access?

If you'd like to use images from your Photos library or album art from Apple Music as wallpaper, the app needs that permission. If you don't plan to use those features, you can deny access and the app will still work with regular folders.

### Can I use multiple folders?

Yes. In the app's settings you can add as many source folders as you like. Images from all of them are mixed into the rotation.

### Which image formats are supported?

JPEG, PNG, HEIC, TIFF, and GIF.

### How do I change how often the wallpaper rotates?

Open the app, go to Settings, and pick an interval — anything from one minute to once a day.

### How do I uninstall the app?

Quit the app from the menu bar, then drag YourPicturesWallpaper from your Applications folder to the Trash. To remove all settings as well, also delete `~/Library/Application Support/YourPicturesWallpaper/`.

### Is my data sent anywhere?

No. The app reads images from your Mac and sets them as wallpaper locally. Nothing is uploaded, no analytics are collected, and no account is required.

### The wallpaper isn't changing — what's wrong?

Three things to check:

1. The app is running (look for its icon in the menu bar).
2. The folder you selected still contains images.
3. macOS hasn't revoked file access — open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Files and Folders and confirm YourPicturesWallpaper is allowed.

If those all look correct, please email me with the details listed under Contact above.

### How do I request a feature?

Email me. I keep a list and prioritize what gets the most requests.
